Recycling Process Overview

The PET bottle‑to‑flake line is engineered to remove impurities step by step so that only clean, stable PET reaches the final packing station. [file:4]

  1. Collection and pre‑sorting of PET bottles by colour, size, and polymer type. [file:4]
  2. Manual and optical sorting to remove metals, PVC, multilayer, and non‑PET items. [file:4]
  3. Label and cap removal using mechanical separation, granulators, and pre‑wash systems. [file:4]
  4. Crushing and shredding of bottles into controlled‑size flakes. [file:4]
  5. Hot washing with friction, detergents, and caustic solution to remove glue and dirt. [file:4]
  6. Float‑sink separation to separate PET from caps, labels, and light plastics. [file:4]
  7. Rinsing and multi‑stage water treatment to remove chemicals and fines. [file:4]
  8. Thermal drying to reach low moisture content suitable for extrusion or spinning. [file:4]
  9. Screening, metal detection, and final inspection before silo storage or bag packing. [file:4]

Who We Are

White Polymer Industries is a PET bottle recycling unit based in Gujarat, India, dedicated to turning local plastic waste into high‑value recycled PET flakes for global markets. [file:4]

From Bottles to Flakes

The plant sources post‑consumer PET bottles from collection centers, scrap traders, and organized waste networks, then converts them into consistently clean, dry flakes suitable for fiber, strap, sheet, and non‑food packaging applications. [file:4]

Each batch is processed through controlled washing, separation, and drying steps to minimize PVC, metal, paper, and other contaminants that can affect downstream production performance. [file:4]

Where Our Flakes Are Used

Cleaned and dried rPET flakes from White Polymer Industries are compatible with a wide range of downstream processes in textiles, packaging, and industry. [file:4]

Polyester Staple Fibre

Spinning & nonwoven applications

A large share of recycled PET flakes is converted into polyester staple fibre (PSF) for garments, home textiles, fillings, geotextiles, and nonwovens. [file:4]

Stable intrinsic viscosity and controlled moisture support smooth spinning, uniform yarn strength, and reduced line interruptions in fibre plants. [file:4]

PET Straps & Monofilament

Packaging and technical yarns

High‑tensile PET straps made from rPET replace steel strapping for pallets, bricks, tiles, and other heavy loads, offering corrosion‑free and safer load securing. [file:4]

Processors also use flakes to produce monofilament yarns for brushes, nets, industrial ropes, and technical textiles. [file:4]

Sheets, Thermoforming & Non‑Food Packaging

rPET sheets and trays

rPET flakes can be extruded into transparent or coloured sheets that are later thermoformed into blisters, clamshells, food‑service trays, and protective packaging for consumer goods. [file:4]

Consistent bulk density and low contamination help maintain clarity and mechanical properties of finished thermoformed products. [file:4]

Other Industrial Uses

Blends and composites

Recycled PET is increasingly used in construction composites, automotive parts, 3D‑printing filaments, and fibre‑reinforced materials where strength‑to‑weight ratio and recyclability are important. [file:4]

These applications extend material life even further, supporting true “waste‑to‑wealth” outcomes in the polymer value chain. [file:4]
